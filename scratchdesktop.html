LASSO JACKS the yard game


Have you ever thought to yourself, "Man, I really wish that I had a yard game where I could Lasso Giant wooden jacks!" Well look no further because you just landed on lassojacks seconday(unoffical) website.


Be an outlaw in your own backyard!

Instructions on how to play

Standard Play: Quick start

  1. 2-4 players
    • Ie. John
    • Jimmy
    • Sally
    • Frank
  2. Sit on a chair in an equally spaced "circle"
  3. Each player gets a lasso
  4. Place the Jacks in the "circle"
  5. Count down from 3 and everyone yells,"YeeHaw!" then begin trying lasso the jacks
  6. Place Lasso jacks infront of your legs
  7. You may lasso jacks from the circle or from in front of other players.
  8. With 4 players, the first to collect 4 jacks in front of them wins!

Setup

  1. Reference the Diagram below
  2. Players 0 sit in a circle 10'-15' across
  3. Place the Jacks X spread out in the circle. This is the open range.
  4. The area directly in front of the players legs is your stable. This is the area where your captured jacks are placed.

Objective

Be the first to capture the winning number of jacks with your lasso.

Winning number of Jacks

  • 4 Players | 4 Jacks
  • 3 Players | 5 Jacks
  • 2 Players | 6 Jacks

    • How to Lasso

    Makin' the Lasso Loop

    1. Feed the 'Tail end' through the 'Honda Loop' until your loop is about twice as big as your intended target.

      2. How to hold the Lasso

    2. With your Non Dominant hand, hold the honda loop.
    3. Slide your Dominant hand down from the honda loop about 12 inches, grabing both your loop and the slack end.
    4. Let go of the Honda loop with your Non dominant hand. You should now be holing your loop up with just your dominant hand.
    5. With your non dominant hand grab the 'Tail end' of your rope and any slack that you don't want getting in the way.

      6. Swinging the Lasso

    6. Begin swinging the loop end of the lasso over your head.
    7. Your wrist should rotate so that your thumb is pointing down when the lasso is behind your head, and up when the lasso is in front of your head.
    8. Your lasso loop should open up if you are rotating your wrist properly, if it's not try swapping the lasso for a stick and swing the stick over your head, this is the motion your wrist should take while using the lasso.

      9. Aim, Release, and Follow through

    9. (If right handed) Aim just to the top left of your intended target.
    10. Release the rope as your are swinging in the forward motion like skipping a rock on a pond.
    11. Point your finger towards the top left of the target after you release
    12. Once your loop is around your target, SUCCESS! You have now lassoed your first jack, pull the tail end to cinch up your loop, pull your jack toward you. Don't pull too hard or the Jack may come back with force and hit you in the shins.
